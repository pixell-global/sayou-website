Philosophy

Three principles of space

Inspired by the 사유의 방 (Room of Quiet Contemplation) at the National Museum of Korea. A 439 sqm space of soil, charcoal, and lacquer, where visitors sit with Pensive Bodhisattva statues beneath a shimmering ceiling of distant light.

비움
Bium
Emptiness as presence

The unfilled space holds meaning precisely because it is unfilled. Every element must earn its place. What we remove matters more than what we add.

여백
Yeobaek
Beauty of empty space

In Korean ink painting, the unpainted area is the most important element. Whitespace is not wasted space. It is where thought completes the experience.

터
Tuh
Character of place

From architect Choi Wook's philosophy. Digital surfaces should have warmth and presence, carrying memory and character. Not neutral containers.

Typography

Museum Silence

The contrast between contemplative serifs and technical sans-serif mirrors the brand's core tension — ancient Korean contemplative practice meets modern developer tooling.

Symbol

The Vault

An elliptical arc, open at the top. Derived from the golden dome edge of 사유의 방 (Room of Quiet Contemplation) at the National Museum of Korea. Containment without closure.
